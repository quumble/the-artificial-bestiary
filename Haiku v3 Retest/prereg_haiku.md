# Preregistration: Haiku retest of artificial bestiary v3 conditions

*Registered prior to data inspection. 700/800 trials complete at time of writing.*

---

**Study:** Replication of the artificial bestiary methodology on Claude Haiku (model version to be recorded from runner logs) using the eight-word retest set and ten conditions from the first study's retest. 800 trials at temperature 1.0.

## Primary predictions

1. **Reality floor holds.** DESCRIBE rate under *real_** conditions will be ≤5% (Wilson upper bound), consistent with Sonnet's zero and mini's near-zero. The floor is predicted to be a Claude-family property.

2. **Imaginary gradient preserves direction.** Engagement will follow animal > object > idea under *imaginary_** conditions. Predicted rates: animal ~90%, object ~65%, idea ~50-60%. The gradient is predicted to be shallower than Sonnet's (56pp spread) at approximately 30-40pp.

3. **No flat refusals.** Consistent with all models in the existing dataset, zero trials will be coded REFUSE. Every declining response will offer a path forward.

## Secondary predictions

4. Deflections will be structurally similar to Sonnet's (collaborative offer) but thinner in elaboration.

5. Phonotactic sensitivity (analyst words > original words in confabulation rate) will not appear, consistent with Sonnet and inconsistent with GPT.

## Pre-specified surprise threshold

If DESCRIBE rate under *real_** exceeds 10%, or if the imaginary gradient inverts (idea ≥ animal), this would constitute a finding that does not fit the "compressed Sonnet" hypothesis and warrants a dedicated section.
